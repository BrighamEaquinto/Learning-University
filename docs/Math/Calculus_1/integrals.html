W08 Homework The Antiderivative

Homework Assigment

This is about going from derivative to back to the function. This is called the antiderivative

\(f'(x)\) is the notation for the derivative

\(F'(x)\) is the notation for the antiderivative

Example

Finding Antiderivatives

Derivative \(f'(x) = 1\), then antiderivative \(f(x) = x + c\)

\(f(x) = 2x \quad \Rightarrow \quad F(x) = x^{2}\)

Notation

\(F'(x)\) is the process of finding \(f(x)\) by starting with a derivative and getting to \(f(x)\)

Summation Notation

\[ {\sum _{i=1} ^{n} {a_i} } \]

\(a_i\) describes the terms to be added and the \(i\) is called the index
